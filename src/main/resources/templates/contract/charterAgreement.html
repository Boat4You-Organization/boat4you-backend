Annex to Charter Agreement #000000/2026

These Terms and Conditions govern the charter agreement between the Broker (Cusmanich d.o.o., trading as Boat4You) and the Charterer in respect of the booking referenced above. By proceeding with payment, the Charterer confirms acceptance of these terms in full.

1.Charter fee and payment

1.1 The charter fee covers the use of the yacht for the charter period stated on page 1, including the equipment listed in the yacht's standard inventory. Optional extras (skipper, hostess, transit log, end-cleaning, fuel, water-toys, pets, etc.) are charged separately and listed on the booking confirmation when applicable.

1.2 Payment is due in the currency stated on the booking confirmation, by bank transfer to the account listed on page 1 of this agreement (or by credit card via the secure payment link the Broker provides). All bank charges are borne by the Charterer.

1.3 The booking is final and binding once the deposit payment is received. If the final balance is not paid by the deadline stated in the payment schedule, the Broker reserves the right to cancel the booking and retain amounts already paid in accordance with Section 12 (Cancellation by the Charterer).

2.Security deposit

2.1 A refundable security deposit is payable at the latest at check-in, in the form and currency requested by the charter agency operating the yacht. The amount of the deposit is shown on the yacht specification sheet.

2.2 The security deposit is held by the charter agency for the duration of the charter and refunded in full at check-out if the yacht and all equipment are returned in the same condition as at check-in, with no damage, no missing items and a full fuel tank.

2.3 The Charterer is liable for the full cost of any damage, loss or repair up to the value of the security deposit. The Broker is not a party to the deposit transaction and accepts no liability for amounts retained by the charter agency.

3.Check-in and check-out

3.1 Check-in is at the time and location stated on page 1. The Charterer must present a valid identity document, the skipper licence and VHF certificate (where the charter is bareboat), and a complete crew list as required by local maritime authorities.

3.2 The yacht is handed over after a joint inventory check. The Charterer is responsible for verifying the inventory and reporting any discrepancies before leaving the marina; later claims cannot be entertained.

3.3 Check-out is at the time and location stated on page 1. Late return entitles the charter agency to charge a penalty equivalent to one additional charter day per started 24-hour period of delay, plus any consequential losses suffered by the next charter.

4.Skipper licence and competence (bareboat charters)

4.1 For bareboat charters the Charterer (or a nominated skipper named in the booking) must hold a valid sailing licence accepted by the competent [(${countryAdjective})] maritime authority, together with a VHF radio operator certificate.

4.2 The Charterer warrants that the skipper has the practical experience to handle the booked yacht in local conditions. The charter agency may refuse to release the yacht if, at check-in, the skipper's competence is reasonably judged to be inadequate; in that case a professional skipper may be required at the Charterer's expense.

5.Skippered and crewed charters

5.1 When a skipper, hostess or full crew is provided, their daily fee is shown on the booking confirmation. The crew's food and drink is paid by the Charterer (typically the equivalent of one additional crew member's provisioning per day, settled directly with the crew).

5.2 The skipper has final authority over the navigation of the yacht, route planning and crew safety. The Charterer is expected to follow the skipper's reasonable instructions at all times.

6.Use of the yacht

6.1 The yacht may be used solely for private leisure cruising. Any commercial use, sub-charter, racing, instruction or carriage of paying passengers is strictly prohibited and voids all insurance cover.

6.2 The maximum number of persons on board (including children and crew) must not exceed the figure stated on the yacht's certificate of registry. The Charterer must keep the crew list up-to-date with the local port authority at all times.

6.3 The Charterer must navigate within the cruising area authorised by the [(${countryAdjective})] maritime authority and the yacht's insurance policy. Crossing into foreign territorial waters requires the prior written consent of the charter agency.

7.Insurance

7.1 The yacht is insured against hull damage, third party liability and crew personal accident as required by local law. The security deposit equals the insurance excess; losses below that amount are borne by the Charterer.

7.2 Personal effects, electronic equipment, cash and valuables of the Charterer and crew are not covered by the yacht insurance. We recommend that the Charterer arranges personal travel and cancellation insurance separately.

7.3 Damage caused by gross negligence, navigation under the influence of alcohol or drugs, sailing in adverse weather despite official warnings, or breach of the cruising area is not covered by insurance and is fully payable by the Charterer.

8.Maintenance, defects and repairs

8.1 The Charterer must keep the yacht and all equipment in good order during the charter, perform routine checks of engine oil, coolant and bilges, and use the equipment with ordinary care.

8.2 Any defect, damage or unusual noise must be reported to the charter agency without undue delay. The agency will organise repair as soon as reasonably practicable. Repairs ordered by the Charterer without the agency's prior consent are not reimbursable.

8.3 If a defect not caused by the Charterer materially impairs the use of the yacht for more than 24 hours and cannot be repaired locally, the Charterer is entitled to a pro-rata refund of the charter fee for the affected period. Consequential losses (flights, accommodation, etc.) are excluded.

9.Weather and force majeure

9.1 The Charterer must observe official weather forecasts and follow port authority instructions. Sailing in winds above the limits set by the charter agency or in waters declared closed by the authorities is prohibited.

9.2 Time lost due to adverse weather, strikes, government action, pandemics, war or other force majeure events does not entitle the Charterer to a refund or extension of the charter, unless the yacht itself becomes unusable for an extended period in which case Section 8.3 applies.

10.Pollution and environmental rules

10.1 The Charterer must comply with all environmental rules of [(${countryAdjective})] waters and any marine protected area visited. Discharging waste, oil or chemicals into the sea is strictly forbidden and subject to penalties imposed by the local authorities, payable by the Charterer.

11.Pets, smoking, parties

11.1 Pets are allowed only on yachts explicitly marked pet-friendly, with the agency's prior written consent and upon payment of the applicable pet fee. Pets are the sole responsibility of the Charterer; any damage they cause is deducted from the security deposit.

11.2 Smoking is not permitted inside the yacht. Open flames (candles, cigarettes) below deck are forbidden.

11.3 Loud parties on board, especially in marinas and anchorages, are prohibited and may lead to immediate termination of the charter without refund.

12.Cancellation by the Charterer

12.1 Cancellation must be communicated in writing to the Broker. The cancellation fee depends on the timing of the request, calculated against the total charter fee:

(a) more than 60 days before check-in: 30% of the charter fee.
(b) 30–60 days before check-in: 50% of the charter fee.
(c) 14–30 days before check-in: 80% of the charter fee.
(d) less than 14 days before check-in: 100% of the charter fee.

12.2 If the cancelled charter is re-sold to another client at the same price and for the same period, the Charterer is refunded the difference between amounts paid and the cancellation fee actually retained, less an administrative fee equal to 10% of the charter fee.

12.3 Travel insurance covering charter cancellation is strongly recommended.

13.Cancellation by the Broker or the charter agency

13.1 If the booked yacht becomes unavailable due to loss, damage or any other reason outside the Broker's control, the Broker will offer a replacement yacht of equivalent specification. If no acceptable replacement is available, all amounts paid are refunded in full and no further claim can be made.

14.Liability

14.1 The Broker acts as an intermediary between the Charterer and the charter agency operating the yacht. Liability for the technical condition of the yacht, the conduct of any crew provided and the performance of services on the day of charter rests with the charter agency. The Broker's liability is limited to the charter fee actually paid by the Charterer.

14.2 The Broker is not liable for indirect or consequential losses, including lost holiday time, missed flights, accommodation costs or non-economic damage.

15.Governing law and dispute resolution

15.1 This agreement is governed by [(${countryAdjective})] law. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this agreement that cannot be settled amicably shall be submitted to the competent court in Split, Republic of Croatia, unless mandatory consumer-protection rules of the Charterer's country of residence require a different forum.

15.2 The Charterer is informed that, as a consumer resident in the European Union, they may also submit complaints through the European Online Dispute Resolution platform at ec.europa.eu/consumers/odr.

16.1 This contract is signed electronically and is valid and binding without a handwritten signature or stamp. Payment of the deposit constitutes acceptance of these terms in full.
